# Code of Conduct

Glacier Engine is a technical community built around curiosity, careful
evidence, and generous collaboration. We want contributors of every experience
level and background to be able to participate safely.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and patient.
- Critique ideas and evidence, not people.
- Welcome questions and explain project-specific terminology.
- Give credit for ideas, investigation, code, documentation, and review.
- State uncertainty and correct mistakes without blame.
- Respect privacy and do not publish credentials, personal data, or private
  benchmark artifacts.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate
intimidation, doxxing, repeated disruption, and sustained bad-faith behavior are
not accepted in project spaces.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through GitHub's
profile contact options. Include links or screenshots when safe to do so. Reports
will be handled as confidentially as practical. Maintainers may edit or remove
content, pause participation, or ban an account when needed to protect the
community.

Good-faith disagreement about technical direction is not a conduct violation.
Retaliation against someone who reports a concern is.
